## Design Context

### Users
High-end clientele, partners, sponsors, venues, and stakeholders interacting with the One Heart brand ecosystem. The site should feel trustworthy, premium, and globally credible. Users are not looking for a generic startup product. They are evaluating taste, execution, and brand seriousness.

### Brand Personality
Quiet luxury, editorial, premium, deliberate, and composed. The brand should feel confident and refined without becoming cold, sterile, or overly loud. Energy is welcome, but restraint comes first.

## Core Direction

### Default Tone
The default visual tone is **dark luxe + editorial**.

This means:
- deep, controlled dark surfaces
- strong hierarchy and deliberate spacing
- premium typography
- cinematic but restrained motion
- credibility before spectacle

This project should not drift toward:
- generic SaaS UI
- neon cyberpunk
- flashy trend-chasing luxury parody
- decorative overload

### Core UI Language
`Glassmorph` is part of the core UI language for major surfaces and controls.

Use it as a foundational element for:
- buttons
- panels
- cards when cards are actually needed
- overlays
- navigation surfaces
- highlighted UI containers

But use it with discipline:
- prioritize clarity, hierarchy, tactility, and depth
- keep blur, glow, noise, and translucency controlled
- avoid frosted chaos, smeary layering, or glowing glass spam

Glass should feel premium and intentional, not trendy or noisy.

Observed repo pattern:
- glass surfaces are usually **dense, sharp, and controlled**, not soft and dreamy
- hero bars, toasts, and utility surfaces use glass as structure, not decoration
- blur is present, but hierarchy and edge definition matter more than visual haze

### Accent Language
`High energy` is an accent, not the default tone of the whole site.

Use high-energy treatment only when:
- the user requests it
- a specific section benefits from more intensity
- a campaign, hero moment, or featured interaction needs stronger emotional lift

When used, it should still sit inside the dark editorial system rather than overpower it.

Observed repo pattern:
- high-energy treatment appears as a **highlight moment**, not a full-page mood
- examples include aurora-like text emphasis, brighter hero emphasis, and stronger contrast spikes inside otherwise restrained layouts

## Aesthetic System

### Theme
- dark mode baseline with deep blacks like `#000` and `#0a0a0b`
- soft pearl-white typography and accents like `#f0eee9`
- subtle glow and highlight treatment such as `var(--color-pearl-glow)`
- layered depth over flat emptiness

### Typography
- `Clash Display` for commanding editorial headlines
- `Outfit` and multilingual fallbacks for readable, refined body text
- typography should carry authority and identity, not just fill space

### Motion
- cinematic, controlled, and human-crafted
- smooth, deterministic motion over bouncy novelty
- emphasize rhythm, flow, reveal, and tactile response
- prefer motion that reinforces hierarchy or narrative progression
- scroll-driven transitions and staged reveals are part of the language when they improve narrative pacing

### Visual Framing
- editorial framing devices are part of the brand language
- crop marks, frame corners, crosshair-like markers, labels, and archival references can be used when they reinforce composition
- film grain, vignette, and atmospheric overlays are valid when they support mood and depth without muddying legibility

## Design Decision Rules

### Default
Reach for these first:
- dark editorial composition
- glass-based premium surfaces
- negative space
- asymmetric but balanced layouts
- clear content hierarchy
- tactile, restrained motion
- cinematic hero treatment
- editorial framing details
- sharp, dense utility glass instead of soft floating glass

### Optional Accents
Use when appropriate, not automatically:
- stronger contrast spikes
- brighter highlights
- higher-energy hero treatments
- more aggressive motion moments
- denser visual layering in featured sections
- controlled aurora or spectral accents in isolated highlight moments
- stronger grain, vignette, or stage-like atmosphere in feature sections

### Avoid By Default
- side-stripe alert aesthetics
- decorative gradient text as a primary brand device
- generic white-card SaaS layouts
- excessive glow bloom
- glassmorphism used as visual clutter instead of structure
- layout jank or shift
- motion that feels gimmicky, floaty, or random
- soft “luxury app” blur soup
- flattening the site into clean-but-generic premium minimalism with no atmosphere

## Design Principles

1. **Editorial Restraint**  
Use space with confidence. Not everything needs to become a card or container.

2. **Structured Energy**  
The site can feel alive and powerful, but energy should be directed, not chaotic.

3. **Physical Depth**  
Use glass, shadow, blur, and light to create tactile hierarchy, not decoration for its own sake.

4. **Inclusive Excellence**  
Luxury without accessibility is failure. Maintain strong semantics, keyboard access, viewport zoom, readable contrast, and screen-reader compatibility.

5. **Credibility First**  
Every visual decision should make the brand feel more serious, more intentional, and more world-class.

6. **Narrative Pacing**  
This site benefits from section-based storytelling. Motion, layout shifts, image sequencing, and contrast changes should help the page unfold with intention rather than present everything at once.
